# Material Repacking Module Process

This document outlines the steps involved in **accessing the Material Repacking module**, **scanning material barcodes**, **viewing material details**, **editing quantities**, and **printing repacked labels** using the Material Repacking Module.

---

## 1. Open Material Repacking Module

Access the Material Repacking screen from the application menu.

- **Step 1.1:** Navigate to **Material Repacking** from the application menu.

- **Step 1.2:** The **Material Repacking** screen will be displayed with material barcode scanning and repacking options.

  <img src="/IMAGE/FGMaterialRepacking/image1.png" width="400" alt="image1">

---

## 2. Scan Material Barcode

Scan the material barcode to retrieve material details for the repacking operation.

- **Step 2.1:** Focus on the **Material Barcode** scan field on the Material Repacking screen.

- **Step 2.2:** Scan the material barcode using the scanner device.

- **Step 2.3:** The system will validate the scanned barcode.

### 2.1 Valid Barcode Scenario

- **Step 2.3.1:** If the scanned barcode is valid, the system will retrieve and display all material details.

- **Step 2.3.2:** Material information including Material Code, Material Description, Available Quantity, Batch No, Location, and other relevant details will be displayed on the screen.

  <img src="/IMAGE/FGMaterialRepacking/image2.png" width="400" alt="image2">
 

### 2.2 Invalid Barcode Scenario

- **Step 2.3.3:** If the scanned barcode is invalid or not found in the system, an error message will be displayed.

- **Step 2.3.4:** The user must scan a valid material barcode to proceed with the repacking operation.

  <img src="/IMAGE/FGMaterialRepacking/image3.png" width="400" alt="image3">
  

---

## 3. View Material Details in Table

Review the scanned material details displayed in the repacking table.

- **Step 3.1:** After scanning the valid barcode, the material details will be automatically displayed in the table below the scanning section.

- **Step 3.2:** The table shows material information.

- **Step 3.3:** Review the material details to identify the item that needs to be repacked.

- **Step 3.4:** Locate the material row in the table that you want to repack.

  <img src="/IMAGE/FGMaterialRepacking/image4.png" width="400" alt="image4">


---

## 4. Select Material Row for Editing

Click on the material row to open the quantity editing dialog.

- **Step 4.1:** Identify the material row in the table that needs quantity adjustment for repacking.

- **Step 4.2:** Click on the desired material row to select it.

- **Step 4.3:** The system will open the **Edit Quantity** dialog box.

- **Step 4.4:** The Edit Quantity box will display the current quantity and allow you to enter new repack quantities.

  <img src="/IMAGE/FGMaterialRepacking/image5.png" width="400" alt="image5">
  

---

## 5. Edit Repack Quantity

Modify the material quantities for the repacking operation.

- **Step 5.1:** The **Edit Quantity** dialog box will be displayed with fields for entering repack quantities.

- **Step 5.2:** Enter the new quantity values for the repacked materials in the designated fields.

- **Step 5.3:** You can enter multiple repack quantities if splitting the material into several packages.

- **Step 5.4:** Verify that all entered quantities are correct and do not exceed the available material quantity.

- **Step 5.5:** Review all quantity values before proceeding with the save operation.

<img src="/IMAGE/FGMaterialRepacking/image5.png" width="400" alt="image5">

---

## 6. Save Repacking Changes

Save the edited quantities and complete the repacking operation.

- **Step 6.1:** After entering all repack quantities in the Edit Quantity dialog, verify that all values are correct.

- **Step 6.2:** Click the **Save** button to save the repacking changes.

- **Step 6.3:** The system will process the repacking operation and update the inventory records.

- **Step 6.4:** A success message will be displayed confirming that the repacking has been completed successfully.

- **Step 6.5:** The Edit Quantity dialog will close and the updated material details will be reflected in the repacking table.

- **Step 6.6:** The repacked materials will be ready for label printing.

 <img src="/IMAGE/FGMaterialRepacking/image6.png" width="400" alt="image6">

---

## 7. Print Repacked Material Labels

Generate and print labels for the repacked materials.

- **Step 7.1:** After successfully saving the repacking changes, locate the **Print Label** button on the screen.

- **Step 7.2:** Click the **Print Label** button to initiate the label printing process.

- **Step 7.3:** The system will display the **Enter Tare Weight** dialog box.

- **Step 7.4:** The dialog will show fields for entering tare weights for the repacked materials:
  - **Tare Weight (New Barcode)**: Enter the tare weight for the newly generated barcode.
  - **Tare Weight (Partial Material)**: Enter the tare weight for the partial or remaining material.

- **Step 7.5:** Enter the appropriate tare weight values in both fields.

- **Step 7.6:** Click the **Submit** button to confirm the tare weights.

- **Step 7.7:** The system will generate and print labels for all repacked materials with the entered tare weights.

- **Step 7.8:** Labels will include material information, repacked quantities, batch details, tare weights, and barcode information.

- **Step 7.9:** A success message will be displayed confirming that the labels have been printed successfully.

- **Step 7.10:** The repacking operation is now complete, and the inventory will be updated with the new repacked material records.
